Common Questions

What is therapy and how can it help me? +

Therapy is a collaborative process that involves talking to a trained professional to explore your feelings, thoughts, and behaviors. It can help you understand yourself better, manage challenges, and develop coping strategies for various life situations.

How long are therapy sessions? +

Therapy sessions typically last between 45 to 60 minutes. The duration can vary based on the type of therapy and your specific needs.

What are the costs associated with therapy? +

Costs for therapy sessions can vary depending on the provider and the type of therapy. It's best to check with your insurance provider for coverage details and to discuss payment options directly with your therapist.

Is my information kept confidential? +

Yes, confidentiality is a fundamental part of the therapeutic process. Your therapist is legally and ethically bound to keep your information private, with certain exceptions for safety and legal obligations.

What can I expect during the first session? +

During your first session, your therapist will likely ask about your reasons for seeking therapy, your background, and any specific concerns you may have. This helps establish a foundation for your work together.
